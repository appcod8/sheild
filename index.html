Why You Can’t Find Sheild Browser on the App Store

Transparency note on App Store keyword visibility and competition.

Even though Sheild Browser is fully approved, it’s effectively invisible in App Store keyword searches.

Search for “browser” on the App Store and you won’t see it. Other apps in the same category appear — but ours doesn’t. Users can only find Sheild Browser if they know the exact name.

Why This Matters

  • Mandatory $99 Annual Fee — Developers must pay to keep apps available, even if Apple is withholding earnings.
  • Opaque Review Process — Updates and bug fixes can be delayed unpredictably, while Apple’s own apps face no such restrictions.
  • Keyword Suppression — Limits discoverability and stifles innovation.

A formal complaint filed with both the FTC and the DOJ about these practices.

“Sheild Browser is fully approved, but buried in App Store search results, making it invisible to users searching for ‘browser.’ Apple’s control effectively erases new competitors, favoring entrenched apps and limiting consumer choice.”
